Flan

Flan is a creamy, custard-based dessert topped with a layer of soft caramel. It is popular in many parts of the world, especially in Latin America, Spain, and the Philippines. Flan is known for its rich, smooth texture and sweet, slightly burnt caramel flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 Eggs
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 can condensed milk
  • 1 can evaporated milk
  • heavy cream

Steps

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat the sugar over medium heat, stirring continually until it melts and turns golden-brown
  2. Quickly pour the caramel into the bottom of an 8–9 inch flan pan or loaf pan, tilting it to coat evenly. Be careful, as the pan will be hot.
  3. Allow the caramel to harden slightly while preparing the custard
  4. In a bowl or blender, combine eggs, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract. Blend or whisk until smooth
  5. Pour the custard mixture over the hardened caramel in the pan
  6. Cover the pan with aluminum foil or a tight-fitting lid
  7. Place the flan pan in a larger baking dish (to act as a water bath).
  8. Pour hot water into the larger dish to come about halfway up the sides of the flan pan.
  9. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 50–90 minutes, depending on pan size. The flan is done when the edges are set, but the center slightly jiggles.
  10. Remove the flan from the water bath carefully and allow it to cool to room temperature.
  11. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to fully set the custard
  12. Run a knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the flan
  13. cover with a serving plate and invert the pan carefully so that the flan releases onto the plate with the caramel on top
  14. after Serve slices as is or garnish with fresh fruit for presentation
